## 1. Executive Summary

- **Capitale:** Lilongwe (administrative) / Blantyre (economic hub)

- **GDP:** $13 milliards (650 USD/capita)

### Opportunité SafeLand

Le Malawi est stabilité exceptionnelle en Afrique subsaharienne : continuité institutionnelle, tech-forward governance, absence de conflit majeur. SafeLand positionne Malawi comme "silicon savanna" leader, déverrouille crédit agricole via collateral land, attire FDI confidence. Plus important : Malawi = template pour Southern Africa (Zimbabwe, Zambia, Mozambique peuvent suivre même blueprint).

### Fit SafeLand

- **Avantage concurrentiel:** Stabilité governance, tech-friendly environment, microfinance infrastructure avancée, hub régional potentiel
- **Taille marché:** €85K Year 1, €280K+ Year 2

- **Délai:** 4.5 mois go-live (décembre 2026)
- **Complexity:** LOW (stable, tech-ready, excellent team potential)

## 2. Contexte Légal & Réglementaire

### Droit Foncier & Réformes

**Cadre moderne (2016 Land Act):**
- Recognition of customary rights (breakthrough = customary tenure now legitimate)
- Individual title registration available
- Dual system: formal + customary (harmonized in 2016 Act)
- Land ownership decentralized to districts (empowered local governance)

**Infrastructure cadastrale:**
- Ministry of Lands, Land Development & Housing (MoLLD) = reformist
- National Lands Commission (emerging, capacity building in progress)
- 28 district land offices (decentralized, mixed capacity)

**Strategic advantage:**
- Modern law = foundation for digital solution
- District-level governance = easier partnership than national (faster approvals)

### Succession & Héritage

- **Droit chrétien:** 90%+ (Anglican, Catholic, Pentecostal mixed)
- **Droit coutumier:** Recognized (Bantu ethnic systems + Islamic minority)
- **SafeLandFridda:** Christian equal distribution + customary variant
- **Simplicity:** Less complex than Benin (more homogeneous population)

### Cadre Crypto & Blockchain

- **Politique:** Tech-friendly government; digital payment systems advanced (mPesa ecosystem)
- **Risk:** Très bas (government actively seeks digital innovation)

### Conformité & Fiscalité

- **Land transfer tax:** 3-4% (registration + transfer)
- **Stamp duty:** 0.5%
- **Property tax:** Municipal (variable)
- **Gestion:** Standard SafeLand model (collect + reverse)

## 4. Roadmap Produit pour le Malawi

### MVP (Décembre 2026) — Urban + Microfinance Focus

**Scope:** 1,000 titres (Lilongwe + Blantyre urban + select rural MFI pilot)

**Modules inclus:**

1. **ERC-721 Land Titles** — Urban formal + microfinance-ready
   - Urban formal cadastre (Lilongwe district data)
   - Microfinance-ready metadata (collateral value, title clarity)
   - Metadata: owner, surface, improvements, estimated value

2. **Escrow Transactions**
   - Urban notary workflow
   - Tax integration (Malawi fiscal rules)

3. **Land-for-Credit API** — Microfinance integration (NEW MODULE)
   - SafeLand ↔ MFI APIs (real-time title verification)
   - Automated collateral assessment (title on-chain = instant verification)
   - Credit decision integration (reduce approval time: 30 days → 5 days)
   - Pilot 50-100 farmer loans (Year 1 proof-of-concept)

4. **Succession** — Christian + customary variant
   - Standard SafeLandFridda rules

- Scale to 2,000+ titles
- 100+ farmer loans via MFI partnerships
- Credit volume: €500K+ (proof of model)

### Year 3 (2028+) — Regional Dominance

## 7. Risques & Mitigations

### Risque #1 — Microfinance Legacy Systems (Moyen)

**Description:** Malawi MFIs use legacy/fragmented systems; API integration complex.

**Impact:** Delayed credit integration, lower Year 1 volume.

**Mitigation:**
- Phase 1 manual (farmer presents title, MFI officer verifies on-chain)
- Phase 2 API (Year 2, negotiate with MFI tech teams)
- Partnership with most progressive MFI first (proof-of-concept)
- Revenue still strong from manual verification (faster approval = value)

---

### Risque #2 — Government Budget Constraints (Moyen)

**Description:** Malawi government cash-strapped (limited fiscal space).

**Impact:** SaaS payment delays, government co-financing not materialized.

**Mitigation:**
- Secure first SaaS payment upfront (quarterly in advance)
- AfDB covers 70%+ costs (reduces government burden)
- Microfinance co-financing (MFIs benefit more, pay directly)

---

### Risque #3 — Climate Vulnerability (Bas-Moyen)

**Description:** Malawi drought-prone (2019, 2022); agricultural disruption.

**Impact:** Farmer collateral value drops, MFI credit reluctance.

**Mitigation:**
- Land-for-credit = GOOD for climate adaptation (collateral access → climate finance)
- Green bonds integration (future, Year 2): climate finance unlocked via land titles
- Messaging: SafeLand = climate resilience tool (not liability)

---

### Risque #4 — Regional Integration Complexity (Moyen)

**Description:** Each Southern Africa country has different land law, cadastre system.

**Impact:** Template doesn't scale 1:1; customization needed per country.

**Mitigation:**
- Malawi = prototype (document all customizations)
- Legal review per country (€5-10K per country, budgeted separately)
- Modular approach (core + country adapters)
- Regional hub team (cross-country knowledge transfer)
